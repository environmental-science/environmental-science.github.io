The student will demonstrate the ability to use scientific skills necessary to identify and analyze environmental issues and to analyze the movement of matter and energy through the biosphere.
The student will demonstrate the ability to describe the structure of an ecosystem and the changes it undergoes, to analyze and recognize the interrelationships in a food chain and a food web and to describe the major biomes and the impact of human involvement and disruption of these biomes.

The student will demonstrate the ability to explain the growth of populations and factors that influence them and to analyze trends in human population growth.
The student will demonstrate the ability to explain the effect of human influences on the atmosphere, water supply and land.

The student will demonstrate the ability to identify renewable and nonrenewable resources and their effect on the environment.
The student will demonstrate the ability to identify the negative impacts of humans on the environment, to explain how citizens can affect environmental policy at each level of government (local, state, and national) and to identify career opportunities in the environmental science field and discuss career information related to the environment.
